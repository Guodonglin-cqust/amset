    Boltzmann transport equation¶

    The Boltzmann transport equation for electrons under a small electric field, F\mathbf{F}F, can be written

    eFℏ∇kfT(k,T)=(δfT(k,T)δt). \frac{e \mathbf{F}}{\hbar} \nabla_\mathbf{k} f_\mathrm{T} (\mathbf{k}, T) = \left ( \frac{\delta f_\mathrm{T} (\mathbf{k}, T)}{\delta t} \right ). ℏeF​∇k​fT​(k,T)=(δtδfT​(k,T)​).

    Under low field conditions, the total distribution function, fTf_\mathrm{T}fT​, is

    fT(k)=f(k)+xg(k), f_\mathrm{T}(\mathbf{k}) = f(\mathbf{k}) + xg(\mathbf{k}), fT​(k)=f(k)+xg(k),

    where xxx is the cosine of the angle between F\mathbf{F}F and k\mathbf{k}k, ggg is the perturbation part of the distribution. and fff is the distribution at equilibrium (Fermi–Dirac distribution), given as

    f=1e(E−EF)/kBT+1 f = \frac{1}{e^{(E-E_\mathrm{F})/k_\mathrm{B}T} + 1} f=e(E−EF​)/kB​T+11​

    Elastic versus inelastic scattering¶

    The perturbation is dependent on the scattering rate sss. The differential scattering rate from state k\mathbf{k}k to k′\mathbf{k}^\primek′ is given as

    s(k,k′)=sinel(k,k′)+sel(k,k′) s(\mathbf{k}, \mathbf{k}^\prime ) = s_\mathrm{inel} (\mathbf{k}, \mathbf{k}^\prime ) + s_\mathrm{el} (\mathbf{k}, \mathbf{k}^\prime ) s(k,k′)=sinel​(k,k′)+sel​(k,k′)

    where the subscripts el\mathrm{el}el and inel\mathrm{inel}inel refer to elastic and inelastic scattering processes, respectively.

    Elastic scattering is completely randomizing and has no effect on fff, i.e.,

    s(k,k′)≡s(k′,k), s(\mathbf{k}, \mathbf{k}^\prime) \equiv s(\mathbf{k}^\prime, \mathbf{k}), s(k,k′)≡s(k′,k),

    whereas ineleastic scattering is not randomizing and does affect fff, i.e.,

    s(k,k′)≠s(k′,k). s(\mathbf{k}, \mathbf{k}^\prime) \neq s(\mathbf{k}^\prime, \mathbf{k}). s(k,k′)​=s(k′,k).

    The non-randomizing behaviour follows from the different rates for emission and absorption processes in inelastic scattering. Accordingly, we define the scattering out rate from k-point, k\mathbf{k}k, as

    sout(k)=∫[s(k,k′)(1−f′)+s(k′,k)f′]dk′, s_\mathrm{out}(\mathbf{k}) = \int \left [ s( \mathbf{k}, \mathbf{k}^\prime ) (1 - f^\prime) + s(\mathbf{k}^\prime, \mathbf{k}) f^\prime \right ] \mathrm{d}\mathbf{k}^\prime, sout​(k)=∫[s(k,k′)(1−f′)+s(k′,k)f′]dk′,

    and scattering in rate to k-point, k\mathbf{k}k, as

    sin(k)=∫Xg′[s(k′,k)(1−f)+s(k,k′)f]dk′, s_\mathrm{in}(\mathbf{k}) = \int X g^\prime \left [ s(\mathbf{k}^\prime, \mathbf{k}) (1 - f) + s(\mathbf{k}, \mathbf{k}^\prime) f \right ] \mathrm{d}\mathbf{k}^\prime, sin​(k)=∫Xg′[s(k′,k)(1−f)+s(k,k′)f]dk′,
